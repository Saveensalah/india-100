Professor K.V. Subramanian, India's former Chief Economic Advisor, spearheaded transformative reforms during the COVID-19 pandemic, balancing supply and demand measures to bolster public capital expenditure. Despite subsequent global challenges, his vision helped India achieve high growth and strong macro fundamentals. He authored three influential Economic Surveys between 2018 and 2021 and earned praise from Prime Minister Narendra Modi for his "academic brilliance and reformatory zeal." A Distinguished Alumnus of IIT-Kanpur and IIM-Calcutta, Prof. Subramanian currently serves as an Executive Director at the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and Professor of Finance (on leave) at the Indian School of Business (ISB)

Know the book

India@100 offers a strategic blueprint for India’s sustained economic growth by leveraging sound economic policies to enable our nation’s rise as a global economic powerhouse by 2047. With clarity and depth, the book describes the journey and multi-pronged reforms needed to achieve that goal. India@100 lays out a compelling and comprehensive vision of India and projects that India can be a $55 trillion economy by 2047 from the present $3.8 trillion. The next two decades present an unparalleled opportunity for India to emerge as a leading economy of that time, a prospect that arises only once in several centuries.
